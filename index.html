üí° Pro Tips from Pet Finders

Click to expand helpful advice

Cats typically hide within a 3-house radius of home. They're scared and won't come when called. Search at night with a flashlight (eyes reflect), leave their litter box outside (they can smell it from far), and set up a humane trap near your home. Check under porches, in garages, up in trees, and inside neighbors' sheds.

Dogs can travel 5-10+ miles in a day when scared. Expand your search radius daily. Post in communities up to 30 miles away. Dogs often follow roads, trails, or water sources. If your dog is friendly, they may approach strangers ‚Äî so flyers in public places work well. If your dog is shy, set up a feeding station with a trail camera.

Nextdoor is #1 for local reach. Facebook lost pet groups have dedicated communities (search "[Your City] Lost and Found Pets"). Ask friends to share ‚Äî engagement boosts visibility. Post on Ring/Neighbors app too. Re-share every 2-3 days to stay visible. Always include: clear photo, location, your phone number.

Dawn and dusk are prime times ‚Äî it's quieter, and scared pets are more likely to come out. For cats especially, search between 10pm-2am when streets are empty. Bring a flashlight to spot eye reflections. Bring smelly food (tuna, hot dogs) and their favorite toy that makes noise.

Call ALL shelters within 60 miles (pets get transported). Visit in person ‚Äî online photos aren't always current. Check with: animal control, police stations, vet offices, pet stores, groomers, dog parks. File reports with microchip company, AKC Reunite, and local Facebook groups. Put a flyer on your own front door!
